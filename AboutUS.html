What exactly is Wildlife Conservation ?

Wildlife conservation refers to the practice of protecting wild species and their habitats in order to maintain healthy wildlife species or populations and to restore, protect or enhance natural ecosystems. Major threats to wildlife include habitat destruction, degradation, fragmentation, overexploitation, poaching, pollution, climate change, and the illegal wildlife trade. The IUCN estimates that 42,100 species of the ones assessed are at risk for extinction.[1] Expanding to all existing species, a 2019 UN report on biodiversity put this estimate even higher at a million species. It is also being acknowledged that an increasing number of ecosystems on Earth containing endangered species are disappearing

Our Mission and How This Website Helps

This website was created to help individuals learn about the significance of wildlife conservation in India. Our platform serves as a gateway to understanding the diversity of Indian wildlife, the sanctuaries established to protect it, and the challenges faced in conservation efforts. By offering information on various topics, we aim to spread awareness and inspire individuals to take action in protecting nature.

How We Educate and Inform

Through articles, research, and updates about wildlife conservation, this website aims to educate visitors about the current state of biodiversity in India. We highlight the importance of each sanctuary, the unique species they protect, and the impact of conservation laws and programs. This knowledge not only helps foster appreciation for India's wildlife but also encourages responsible behavior that contributes to conservation.

Connecting with Conservation Efforts

We also provide contact information for various conservation organizations, enabling people to connect directly with groups working on the front lines. By reaching out, users can volunteer, donate, or participate in local initiatives, making a tangible difference in the conservation landscape.
